3. Member Ownership and Capital

The Member owns one hundred percent (100%) of the Company. The Member may contribute capital, property, or services as the Member determines and may record such contributions in the Company’s records.

4. Management

The Company is managed by the Member. The Member has full authority to act on behalf of the Company, including signing contracts, opening accounts, hiring service providers, and taking actions consistent with the Company’s purpose.

5. Profits, Losses, and Distributions

Profits and losses will be allocated to the Member. The Member may take distributions at any time, subject to maintaining sufficient funds to satisfy Company obligations.

6. Records and Banking

The Company will maintain reasonable business records and may maintain a separate bank account. Company and personal funds should be kept separate to preserve limited liability protections.

7. Liability and Indemnification

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Member is not personally liable for Company debts solely by reason of being a member. The Company will indemnify the Member for actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company.

8. Taxes

Unless otherwise elected, the Company will be treated as a disregarded entity for federal tax purposes, and Company income and expenses will be reported by the Member. The Member may elect a different tax classification if desired.

9. Dissolution

The Company may be dissolved by the Member. Upon dissolution, the Company will pay liabilities, then distribute remaining assets to the Member.

10. Governing Law and Disputes

This Agreement is governed by the laws of California, without regard to conflict-of-law principles.
